What is Faang?

FAANG refers to the five prominent American technology companies: Facebook, Amazon, Apple, Netflix, and Alphabet (GOOG). The term was coined by Jim Cramer, the television host of CNBC's Mad Money in 2013, calling these companies "totally dominant in their markets." Until 2017, FANG was limited to Facebook, Amazon, Netflix, and Alphabet. Another variant of this acronym is "FANGAM," which includes Microsoft.
